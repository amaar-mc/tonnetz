# Architecture

`tonnetz` is a set of small pure modules. A triad is `{ root, quality }` where root is a
pitch class in 0..11 and quality is "major" or "minor". A major triad on root r has
pitch classes `{r, r+4, r+7}`; a minor triad has `{r, r+3, r+7}`.

## The transformations

Each transformation maps a triad to the triad that shares two of its three pitch classes
across the relevant edge of the Tonnetz.

- P (Parallel): keep the root and fifth, flip the quality. `{r, major} <-> {r, minor}`.
- L (Leittonwechsel): keep the third and fifth.
  Major: `{r, major} -> {r+4, minor}` (C major to E minor).
  Minor: `{r, minor} -> {r-4, major}` (A minor to F major).
- R (Relative): keep the root and third.
  Major: `{r, major} -> {r+9, minor}` (C major to A minor).
  Minor: `{r, minor} -> {r+3, major}` (A minor to C major).

The major-triad and minor-triad cases differ, which is the most common source of bugs in
ad hoc implementations. Each transformation is an involution, and each preserves exactly
two common tones; both facts are tested.

Two named compound transformations are also provided. N (Nebenverwandt) sends a major
triad to its subdominant minor (`{r, major} -> {r+5, minor}`) and a minor triad to its
dominant major (`{r, minor} -> {r+7, major}`); it equals applying R, then L, then P. S
(Slide) shifts the root by a semitone and flips quality (`{r, major} -> {r+1, minor}`,
`{r, minor} -> {r-1, major}`), keeping the shared third; it equals applying L, then P,
then R. Both are involutions preserving one common tone, and the tests check each against
its composition across all 24 triads.

## The group

P, L, and R generate the dihedral group of order 24, which acts simply transitively on
the 24 consonant triads. That is why `pathBetween` always finds a route: a breadth-first
search over the 24 triads with the three operations as edges is guaranteed to reach any
target, and the first time it reaches the target it has found a shortest path.

## Cycles

- `hexatonicCycle` alternates P and L. The orbit has six triads that share a single
  hexatonic (augmented) collection.
- `octatonicCycle` alternates R and P. The orbit has eight triads that share a single
  octatonic collection.

Both are generated by stepping the alternating operations from the seed and collecting
the triads until the cycle would repeat.

## The lattice

`pitchAtLattice(fifths, majorThirds)` returns `(7 * fifths + 4 * majorThirds) mod 12`.
Moving one step along the fifths axis adds a perfect fifth; one step along the
major-thirds axis adds a major third. Because pitch classes are taken modulo 12, the
lattice is a torus and many coordinates map to the same pitch class.

## Why pure TypeScript

The transformations and the group are small and exactly specified. Implementing them with
no dependencies keeps the library usable in any environment, including the browser
visualizers that are the natural home for Tonnetz tools.
